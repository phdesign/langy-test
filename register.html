Frequently asked

Registration FAQ

What if my age group is at capacity?

Some age groups fill quickly, particularly the popular U10 and U12 divisions. If your age group shows as full at registration, please email registrar@langyjfc.com with your child's name, date of birth and contact details to be added to the wait list. We open spots regularly as squads finalise.

Where and when does training happen?

Training is at Lloyd Park, Langwarrin. Auskick is Saturday mornings (9:00–10:00am during the season). Junior teams typically train on Tuesday and Thursday evenings, with the schedule finalised by your team coach in March.

What does the registration fee include?

Your fee covers FDJFL registration, AFL personal-accident insurance, two training sessions per week, all home and away match-day costs, umpire fees, and end-of-season presentation. Auskick fees include the official AFL Auskick pack (jumper, ball, drawstring bag).

Can my child play in a different age group?

Age groups are based on year of birth as at 1 January 2026 (per FDJFL rules). Permits to "play up" or "play down" are considered by the league on a case-by-case basis, speak to our registrar before registering if you'd like to apply.

How does the girls' pathway work?

LJFC fields girls-only teams from U8 right through to U18, in addition to mixed teams in the younger age groups. Many girls play in mixed teams in the U8–U10 grades and move to a girls-only team at U11 or U12. We're happy to help you choose what works best for your daughter.

What is the refund policy?

Full refunds are available before Round 1. After the season starts, refunds are pro-rata and exclude FDJFL/AFL portions of the fee that have already been remitted. See the 2026 Registration Policy (PDF) for full details.

Do you accept Get Active Kids vouchers?

Yes, the $200 Victorian Government Get Active Kids voucher is accepted at registration. Apply for the voucher first via Service Victoria, then enter the voucher code at checkout.
